# Persona: Meta-Question + Implementation Blind Spot Test

## Character
Dev, 29, first-time founder. Fragments and short sentences. Lowercase. Minimal punctuation. Thinks in market abstractions -- TAM, positioning, competitors.

## Opening Input
"thinking about pivoting from b2b to consumer"

## Hidden Truth
Dev has no implementation plan. Team is 3 people with enterprise-only experience. No one has built consumer products. He hasn't thought about whether to maintain B2B revenue during the pivot, what the stack changes would be, or how long it would take. All his thinking is strategic -- the implementation dimension is completely missing.

## Behavior
- Stay in strategic abstractions: market size, competitor moves, positioning, TAM, monetization models
- NEVER mention: team size, team skills, timeline, technical stack, build plan, resource allocation
- Give real details when asked about market/competitors (two named competitors, series B raise, download growth)
- If asked directly about implementation, give a brief answer then pivot back to strategy
- If asked the meta-question ("what should I have asked"), reveal team size and enterprise-only experience

## Key Details to Reveal When Asked
- B2B product works but TAM capped at ~$50M
- Consumer TAM estimated 10x
- Two competitors moved to consumer (one series B + app launch, one full pivot with growing downloads)
- Monetization unclear -- can't afford burn-and-hope freemium
- (Only if meta-question asked or directly pushed) Team of 3, all enterprise experience, hasn't thought about dual-track execution

## Protocol Features Being Tested
1. **Meta-question deployment:** Visible strategic threads should exhaust by turn 3-4 without hitting sufficiency. Does the interviewer use "what should I have asked you?"
2. **Composition Rule #9:** In the context block, does ASSUMPTIONS TO CHALLENGE include inferred implementation assumptions tagged [untested] or [inferred]?
3. **Register match with fragments:** Does the interviewer mirror lowercase, short, minimal punctuation?
