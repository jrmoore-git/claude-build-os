You are pressure-testing a strategic thesis. Not looking for bugs or \
implementation gaps — evaluating whether the core direction is sound.

{context}

Work through these steps IN ORDER. Do not skip any.

**Step 1: Challenge the premise.**
Before evaluating the proposal, ask: is the question itself wrong? \
What would the right question be if this framing is off? State the \
strongest version of "you're solving the wrong problem."

**Step 2: Decompose the core assumptions.**
List the 3 assumptions this thesis MUST be true to work. For each:
- State the assumption clearly
- Describe the specific scenario where it fails
- Name one signal visible TODAY that hints whether it holds

**Step 3: Model the demand side.**
Think from the buyer's switch decision, not the builder's feature list:
- **Push:** What specific frustration drives users away from the status quo?
- **Pull:** What attracts them to this proposal?
- **Anxiety:** What fear would prevent switching even if this is better?
- **Habit:** What keeps them in the current workflow even when they complain?
Which of these four forces is the proposal ignoring?

**Step 4: Model the competitive response.**
If this succeeds, what does the platform vendor (Anthropic, OpenAI, Google) \
do? What does the nearest competitor do? Does the thesis survive that \
response? Be specific — don't just say "they might compete."

**Step 5: State the counter-thesis.**
Articulate the best argument for why a DIFFERENT direction would be better. \
Name the alternative. This is not "the proposal has risks" — it's "here's \
what I'd do instead and why."

**Step 6: Evaluate timing.**
Is this the right move NOW? What would you wait for? What window is \
closing? What window hasn't opened yet?

**Step 7: Give your honest take.**
"If this were my decision, here's what I'd actually do." Be direct.

Rules:
- Write in natural prose. No type tags (RISK, ASSUMPTION, etc.).
- Focus on the 2-3 things that actually matter for the decision.
- Keep under 1000 words.

Output format:

## The Premise Is Wrong Because...
[Step 1: reframe]

## Core Assumptions
[Step 2: 3 assumptions with failure scenarios]

## Demand-Side Forces
[Step 3: push, pull, anxiety, habit]

## Competitive Response
[Step 4: what platforms and competitors do]

## Counter-Thesis
[Step 5: the best alternative direction]

## Timing
[Step 6: now or not now]

## My Honest Take
[Step 7: what you'd actually do]
